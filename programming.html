I am not a programmer. I dabble in programming.

Started with Pascal and BASIC in the previous century. Learned basics of C at the universtity. Had a short affair with Ruby when Rails was popular. Chunky bacon. Tried Objective-C but gave up easily. Drew LabView because it connected to all the measurement gear around. Wrote some C++ for my beloved DSP processor. Did MATLAB for many years because of academic pressure, got good at it, dropped it for Julia. Licked some LISP but it tased of unclosed parentheses. Got my head blown by Haskell. Currently APL curious because it looks beautiful but not curious enough to actually do anything about it.

I treat programming languages like bicycles. You can never have too many.
